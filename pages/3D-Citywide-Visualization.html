Analyzing the Potential Impact of Rising Sea Levels on Miami Beach

Planning a Resilient Future – 3D City-Wide Visualization

This 3D city-wide visualization illustrates how projected sea level rise could impact buildings and infrastructure across Miami Beach, using color-coded structures to indicate varying levels and timing of flood exposure. By integrating building footprints with elevation data and future sea-level scenarios, the map highlights vulnerable coastal corridors, dense urban areas, and zones requiring early adaptation measures.

Technical Implementation

  • • Raster–vector integration combining elevation models and building footprints
  • • Scenario-based modeling for 2030, 2050, and 2090 projections
  • • Spatial classification of flood exposure levels
  • • Advanced 3D cartographic visualization for stakeholder communication

Project Purpose

Designed to communicate climate risk clearly for both technical and non-GIS audiences, supporting evidence-based resilience planning and infrastructure adaptation strategies.

Impact & Value

Risk Identification

Highlighted vulnerable coastal corridors and high-density urban zones under projected sea-level rise conditions.

Scenario Planning

Enabled comparison of short-, mid-, and long-term climate impact projections to support phased adaptation strategies.

Decision Support

Provided a clear 3D spatial narrative for planners, engineers, and policymakers to prioritize resilience investments.
